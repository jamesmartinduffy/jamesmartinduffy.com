What I’m Working On:

GoSnapShop.com is an ecommerce platform that allows Korean customers to buy fashion products directly from US merchants, avoiding the markups charged by importers and local distributors.

The platform is 100% automated. We use a combination of data feeds, product APIs, and screen scraping to update thousands of products from multiple merchants every day. When the user completes a purchase, it launches a “purchase robot” that navigates to the merchant’s website and purchases the product, arranges the cheapest international shipping through a third party shipping company (around $10 for a pair of jeans), and arranges payment of any customs fees if applicable.

To the user, this makes shopping from overseas stores as simple as a one-click purchase from an ecommerce store in their native language. To Casual Steps, being fully automated means we can scale up to handle unlimited merchants, products, and orders with minimal effort.

Technologies (Nerd Stuff): The site is built on Ruby on Rails and the open-source ecommerce platform Spree Commerce. For scraping / automation, we use node.js and headless browsing with phantom.js. Redis and RabbitMQ are the glue that hold the app together. We use Vagrant and Ansible for development environment and server management.
